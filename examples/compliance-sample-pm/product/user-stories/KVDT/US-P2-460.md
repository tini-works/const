### User Story

As a **System**, the system must validate the patient's postal code against the KBV PLZ-Stammdatei when captured via Ersatzverfahren with KTAB=00,
so that only valid postal codes are transmitted in the ADT billing under the Wohnortprinzip.

### Compliance Context

Under the Wohnortprinzip, the patient's PLZ in the billing file is administrative data used for allocation. Invalid postal codes cause billing rejections. The real PLZ must always be preserved in the patient record for medical and correspondence purposes.

### Acceptance Criteria

**Scenario 1: Valid PLZ exists in Stammdatei**
Given the Ersatzverfahren is used (no Einlesedatum FK 4109) and KTAB FK 4106 = "00"
AND the patient's PLZ exists in the PLZ-Stammdatei (reference field 0150)
When the ADT billing is generated
Then the PLZ is transferred into field 3112 or 3121

**Scenario 2: PLZ not found — warning and correction required**
Given the Ersatzverfahren is used with KTAB = "00"
AND the patient's PLZ does not exist in the PLZ-Stammdatei
When the system checks the PLZ
Then a warning is issued that the PLZ does not exist in the PLZ-Stammdatei
AND the invalid PLZ is not transferred into the ADT billing

**Scenario 3: Domestic patient — fallback to practice PLZ**
Given the patient's PLZ is invalid and the patient resides domestically
When no valid PLZ can be determined
Then the PLZ of the treating physician's practice location (Feld 0215, Satzart "besa") is transferred into field 3112 or 3121

**Scenario 4: Foreign patient — PLZ dummy "99999"**
Given the patient's PLZ is invalid and the patient resides abroad
When the ADT billing is generated
Then the PLZ-Dummy "99999" is written into field 3112
AND the real foreign PLZ is preserved in the patient documentation

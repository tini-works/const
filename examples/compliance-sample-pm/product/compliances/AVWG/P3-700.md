### Requirement

The prescribing software must support both the printing of paper-based prescriptions and the creation of electronic prescriptions pursuant to § 86 SGB V.

### Rationale

The prescribing of medicinal products and other products included in the pharmaceutical supply pursuant to § 31 SGB V is carried out by default as an electronic prescription in accordance with the legal requirements. Narcotics and T-prescription medicines are excluded from this.

### Acceptance Criteria

1. The prescribing software must support the following variants for issuing prescriptions ([**P3-621**](P3-621.md), [**O3-622**](O3-622.md), [**O3-623**](O3-623.md), [**P3-624**](P3-624.md)):
   - Electronic prescription per [**P3-710**](P3-710.md)
   - Paper-based prescription per [**P3-720**](P3-720.md)
2. The contract physician must be able to decide, based on the legal requirements, which variant per acceptance criterion 1 they wish to use.
   - The prescribing software offers the user the electronic prescription per [P3-710](P3-710.md) as the default.
3. The prescribing software must be able to switch between the variants per acceptance criterion 1 without requiring a renewed selection of already selected products or of already entered prescription information for active ingredient, compound, and free-text prescriptions.
4. The prescribing software must, when creating a prescription in electronic form, enable an additional printout for the patient to be provided at the request of the contract physician, in accordance with the requirements of the Technical Annex for electronic pharmaceutical prescriptions.

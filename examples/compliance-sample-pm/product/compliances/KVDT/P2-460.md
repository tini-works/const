### Requirement

The postal code (PLZ) of the patient to be transferred in field 3112 and/or 3121 must be checked for existence against the **PLZ-Stammdatei der KBV** only when the PLZ was manually captured through the **Ersatzverfahren**; that is, the ADT-Abrechnungsdatensatz contains no Einlesedatum (FK 4109) and additionally the Kostenträger-Abrechnungsbereich (KTAB, FK 4106) is defined as **"00"**.

If an Ersatzverfahren exists and an existence check is required, the following case distinction must be observed:

**Case a)**
If the patient's PLZ present in the billing software exists in the PLZ-Stammdatei (reference field 0150), it must be transferred into field 3112 or 3121 of the ADT-Abrechnungsdatensatz. The existence check is performed regardless of whether it is a domestic or foreign PLZ.

**Case b)**
If the patient's PLZ present in the billing software **does not exist** in the PLZ-Stammdatei, then:

1. A warning must be issued that the patient's PLZ does not exist in the PLZ-Stammdatei and must be changed accordingly.
2. The existing PLZ must not be transferred into the ADT-Abrechnungsdatensatz.
3. The user must make a suitable correction as follows:
   a) If the patient resides domestically, a valid PLZ corresponding to the patient must be transferred into the ADT-Abrechnungsdatensätze that exists in the PLZ-Stammdatei der KBV. If no "valid" PLZ can be determined, then the PLZ of the treating physician's practice location is transferred into field 3112 or 3121 (PLZ/PostfachPLZ) of the ADT-Abrechnungsdatensatz (Inhalt Feld 0215, Satzart "besa").
   b) If the patient resides abroad, then the PLZ-Dummy "99999" (five nines) is written into field 3112 (PLZ) of the ADT-Abrechnungsdatensatz. (The foreign PLZ of the patient is replaced by 99999 in this case.)

### Notes

The PLZ transferred into the ADT-Abrechnungsdatensatz under the Wohnortprinzip is purely administrative information.

That is, the physician must NOT replace the patient's PLZ in the medical patient documentation (e.g. for postal correspondence or worse: for emergencies) with a pseudo number. The application software must ensure that in these cases the real PLZ of the patient is stored in the patient documentation (= patient record). This real PLZ is also to be used for printing the personal data field of contractual physician forms.

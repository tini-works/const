### Requirement

The software must inform the user that services will not be reimbursed as a Hybrid-DRG service if the difference between the date in field 5028 (Service Start Date) and the date in field 5029 (Service End Date) is greater than 2 days.

### Rationale

In outpatient treatment, the start and end dates may not be more than two days apart. Once the difference exceeds two days, no grouping into a Hybrid-DRG occurs.

### Acceptance Criteria

1. If, when recording Hybrid-DRG services, the difference between the date in field 5029 (Service End Date) and the date in field 5028 (Service Start Date) is greater than 2, the software must display a warning message indicating that for Hybrid-DRG services, the start and end dates may not be more than two days apart.

### Condition

Implementation of Hybrid-DRG billing according to the record type HDRG.

### Notes

The determining factor for calculating the length of stay is the number of bed days. Bed days are the admission day and each additional day of the hospital stay, excluding the transfer or discharge day from the hospital; if a patient is admitted and transferred or discharged on the same day, that day counts as the admission day.

**Example:**
1. Admission on: 17.02.2026 (FK 5028), Discharge on: 18.02.2026 (FK 5029) => no warning is displayed (Length of stay = 1 day)
2. Admission on: 17.02.2026 (FK 5028), Discharge on: 19.02.2026 (FK 5029) => no warning is displayed (Length of stay = 2 days)
3. Admission on: 17.02.2026 (FK 5028), Discharge on: 20.02.2026 (FK 5029) => a warning **is** displayed (Length of stay = 3 days)

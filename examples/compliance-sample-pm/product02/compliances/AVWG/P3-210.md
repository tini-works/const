### Requirement

Advertising is only permitted in the form of advertising windows. These must be clearly identifiable by labelling them as "Advertising" and must not be misleadingly presented as information windows. They must be directly recognisable as advertising. Professional content and advertising must be clearly delimited from each other and must not be intermixed. No function may be placed behind an advertisement that directly or indirectly leads to, for example, the issuance of a prescription, a change on the prescription (e.g. changing the "aut idem" status), or a transfer to the in-house pharmacy. No function may be placed behind an advertisement that has a direct or indirect influence on default settings or the configuration of the prescription software (e.g. filter settings for search functions).

Advertising must not distract from the prescribing process by its appearance, extent, or presentation. It must be ensured that the functionalities of the prescription software can be used without restriction even when advertising overlays are present. Advertising overlays must not interrupt the workflow.

If advertising is clicked and thereby content is opened, e.g. websites, that are not covered by the requirements catalogue for prescription software pursuant to § 73 SGB V, the following notice must be displayed: "You are now leaving your prescription software. Please note that this link opens content, e.g. websites, to which the requirements catalogue for prescription software pursuant to § 73 SGB V does not apply."

Advertising or notices that explicitly or implicitly encourage ticking the "aut idem" checkbox are impermissible.

The integration of manufacturer-specific product catalogues from which a prescription can be issued directly or indirectly is impermissible. Animated advertising is not permitted.

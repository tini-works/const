### Requirement

The prescribing software must ensure a legally compliant prescription conforming to the BMV-Ae on the pharmaceutical prescription form (Muster 16) as well as on BtM and T-prescription forms.

### Rationale

The prescribing of medicinal products and other products included in the pharmaceutical supply pursuant to § 31 SGB V is carried out in accordance with the legal requirements. Legal and sub-legal regulations, such as the BMV-Ae, establish among other things formal requirements for how paper-based prescriptions are to be issued.

### Acceptance Criteria

1. For printing the personal data field, the Technical Annex to Annex 4a (BMV-Ae) Chapter "2.3 Bedruckung des Personalienfeldes" must be observed.
2. The prescribing software must be able to print up to three prescription lines per prescription form.
3. Acceptance criterion 2 does not apply to compound prescriptions ([**O3-623**](O3-623.md)), replacement prescriptions ([**P3-630**](P3-630.md)), and multiple prescriptions.
4. The prescribing software must ensure that the simultaneous use of a pharmaceutical prescription form for prescribing both medical devices and medicinal products is not permitted. The prescribing software must inform the contract physician during printing that separate prescription forms are to be used.
5. The system must automatically scale the font size to display the information completely -- the minimum font size is 10 pt. One of the monotype fonts Courier or Courier New should be used. When using dot matrix printers, the NLQ mode is additionally a minimum requirement.
6. The available space on the prescription form is to be utilized as effectively as possible. The goal is to display three prescriptions (acceptance criterion 2). If a dosage is specified on the prescription ([**P3-625**](P3-625.md)) and as a result the information listed in mandatory functions [**P3-721**](P3-721.md) and [**K3-722**](K3-722.md) cannot be displayed in two lines in the printout, the prescribing software may use multiple lines.
   - In this case, the prescribing software must appropriately inform the contract physician about how many prescription forms are needed, and provide the option to abbreviate the information to display up to three prescriptions (where applicable using acceptance criterion 7).
7. The software may assist the contract physician in abbreviating prescription information where it cannot be printed completely in the available space.
   - The prescribing software must ensure that the data from the pharmaceutical master data and the dosage are not abbreviated.
   - The abbreviated content proposed by the prescribing software must be visible to and confirmed by the contract physician.
8. The prescribing software ensures that the "Datum" (Date) field of the personal data field is filled with a valid date value.

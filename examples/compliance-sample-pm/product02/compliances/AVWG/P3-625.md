### Requirement

The prescribing software must enable the specification of dosage information on a prescription.

### Rationale

The Arzneimittelverschreibungsverordnung (AMVV) requires in § 2 and the BtMVV in § 9 Absatz 1 Nummer 5 the specification of the dosage on the prescription or an indication that a medication plan or a written dosage instruction is available. This does not apply when the prescribed pharmaceutical is delivered directly to the prescribing person.

### Acceptance Criteria

1. The prescribing software must, for prescriptions of prescription-only pharmaceuticals (Merkmal 011) from the pharmaceutical master data ([**P3-621**](P3-621.md)), active substance prescriptions ([**O3-622**](O3-622.md)), and free-text prescriptions ([**P3-624**](P3-624.md)), offer a selection of the following three options:
   1. An input field for dosage information on the prescription (acceptance criteria 3 and 4), or
   2. The marker indicating the availability of a medication plan or a written dosage instruction (acceptance criterion 5), or
   3. No dosage information, as not required. This applies when, for example, no dispensing to the patient occurs (e.g., for consultation-hour supplies).
   - The prescribing software prevents this selection (acceptance criterion 1.3) for prescriptions of narcotics (Merkmal 012).
   - The prescribing software notifies the user with a notice for every prescription without dosage information that omitting dosage information is only permitted in the following cases:
     - Consultation-hour supplies
     - Dispensing to the prescribing physician
   - If the prescription is marked as consultation-hour supplies, the display of the notice may be omitted.
   - The notice must not interrupt the prescribing process.
2. The prescribing software must ensure that for prescriptions of prescription-only pharmaceuticals, one of the options defined under acceptance criterion 1 is specified by the contracted physician.
3. The prescribing software must enable the specification of a dosage via a free-text field as follows:
   - For a paper-based prescription, the printout is placed after the prescribed product at the end of the prescription line using the characters ">>" ... "<<".
     - Examples:
       - `Ramipril XYZ-Pharma 2,5 mg 20 Tbl. N1 PZN01234567 >>1-0-0<<`
       - `Ramipril XYZ-Pharma 2,5 mg 20 Tbl. N1 PZN01234567 >>1-0-0-0<<`
   - For an electronic prescription, the entry is made via the element MedicationRequest.dosageInstruction.text and the entry MedicationRequest.dosageInstruction.extension:Dosierungskennzeichen.value[x]:valueBoolean is set to "true" in profile KBV_PR_ERP_Prescription.
4. The prescribing software may optionally enable a structured entry of a dosage.
   - For a paper-based prescription, the printout is placed after the prescribed product at the end of the prescription line using the characters ">>" ... "<<".
   - For an electronic prescription, the entry is made via the element MedicationRequest.dosageInstruction.text in profile KBV_PR_ERP_Prescription.
5. The prescribing software must enable the indication that a medication plan or a written dosage instruction is available.
   - For a paper-based prescription, the printout is placed after the prescribed product at the end of the prescription line using ">>Dj<<" (meaning: yes, a medication plan or a written dosage instruction is available).
     - Example: `Ramipril XYZ-Pharma 2,5 mg 20 Tbl. N1 PZN01234567 >>Dj<<`
   - For a paper-based prescription of narcotics (Merkmal 012), the printout is placed after the prescribed product at the end of the prescription line using ">>gemaess schriftlicher Anweisung<<".
   - For an electronic prescription, the marking is made via the element MedicationRequest.dosageInstruction.extension:Dosierungskennzeichen.value[x]:valueBoolean set to "false" in profile KBV_PR_ERP_Prescription.
6. When for a paper-based prescription the marking ">>Dj<<" or for narcotics ">>gemaess schriftlicher Anweisung<<" has been set per acceptance criterion 5, since a written dosage instruction is available, the prescribing software must ensure that no dosage information per acceptance criteria 3 and 4 is recorded on the prescription. When dosage information per acceptance criterion 3 or 4 has been recorded, the prescribing software must ensure that no marking ">>Dj<<" or for narcotics ">>gemaess schriftlicher Anweisung<<" is set per acceptance criterion 5.
7. For prescriptions of non-prescription pharmaceuticals (Merkmal 010) from the pharmaceutical master data, the selection per acceptance criterion 1 may be offered.
8. Switching between the three options per acceptance criterion 1 must be possible without requiring a new selection of already selected products or re-entry of already entered prescription information for active substance prescriptions.
9. A default setting by the prescribing software of both the option under acceptance criterion 1 Nr. 2 (availability of a medication plan or a written dosage instruction, see acceptance criterion 5) and the option under acceptance criterion 1 Nr. 3 (no dosage information) is generally not permitted. Input must be actively performed by the physician. Exceptions include, for example, vaccine prescriptions issued in the patient's name and dispensed to the patient. Storage per acceptance criterion 10 is also permitted.
10. The dosage information or the marking indicating the availability of a medication plan or a written dosage instruction may be stored. Transfer in the context of repeated prescriptions ([**P3-120**](P3-120.md)) for a patient is permitted.

### Notes

Compounding prescriptions are excluded from this regulation, as a **Gebrauchsanweisung** (instructions for use) must always be specified instead of dosage information. Dosage information per [**P3-625**](P3-625.md) is therefore not intended for compounding prescriptions.

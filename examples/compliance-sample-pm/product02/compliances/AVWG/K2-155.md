### Requirement

Entries in the Hausapotheke must be stored at package level and/or free-text level.

For finished pharmaceuticals and other products included in pharmaceutical care pursuant to § 31 SGB V, the PZN (001) of the product must be stored in the Hausapotheke at package level at minimum, if available. Further information on this PZN to be displayed during prescribing from the Hausapotheke can be found under [**P3-120**](P3-120.md).

For products that are not uniquely identified by a PZN (e.g., compounded prescriptions), at least a free-text field must be provided for storing these in the Hausapotheke.

When transferring a product from the Hausapotheke to a prescription, the specifications from Chapter 3.9 must be observed.

### Rationale

To provide the user with easy access to their frequently prescribed pharmaceuticals, the software manufacturer can offer the user a so-called Hausapotheke.

### Acceptance Criteria

1. For products selected from the Price and Product Directory and assigned a PZN (001, 201, 401, 501, 601), the PZN of the product must be stored in the Hausapotheke at package level at minimum. Note: The further information on this PZN to be displayed during prescribing from the Hausapotheke is defined under [**P3-120**](P3-120.md).
2. For products not uniquely identified by a PZN and prescribed via a free-text prescription, the information defined per requirement [P3-624](P3-624.md) is to be stored.
3. For products not uniquely identified by a PZN and prescribed via an active ingredient prescription per [O3-622](O3-622.md), the information defined per requirement [O3-622](O3-622.md) is to be stored.
4. For products not uniquely identified by a PZN and prescribed via a compounded prescription per [O3-623](O3-623.md), the information defined per requirement [O3-623](O3-623.md) is to be stored.
5. When transferring a product from the Hausapotheke to a prescription, the specifications from Chapter 3.9 must be observed.

### Notes

Condition: Implementation of requirement [**O2-145**](O2-145.md).

Under Hausapotheke, practice-related BSNR/NBSNR or physician-related LANR product lists are to be understood, from which prescriptions can be made. A prescribing software may contain one or more of these product lists. Therefore, the following requirements are conditional mandatory functions. The product lists are stored in the prescribing software. As soon as a prescribing software has a Hausapotheke, the functions [**K2-150**](K2-150.md) through [**K2-160**](K2-160.md) must be implemented.

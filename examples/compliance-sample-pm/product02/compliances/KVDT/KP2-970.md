### Requirement

If services for an approved psychotherapy have been recorded and at quarter's end a remaining contingent for the approved psychotherapy exists and a relapse prevention can follow, the software shall notify the user about the specification of Pseudo-GOP 88131.

### Rationale

This requirement results from section 17 para. 7 of the Psychotherapy Agreement (Annex 1 BMV-Ae).

### Acceptance Criteria

1. The software must notify the user at least during billing creation about the possible recording and transfer of a "Termination notification with Pseudo-GOP 88131" if:
   b) the duration of the already performed treatment amounts to at least 40 therapy units and
   c) in the billing quarter, approved psychotherapy services have been recorded and
   d) no Pseudo-GOP 88130 or 88131 has been recorded and
   e) the psychotherapy is not interrupted.
2. The software may make the notification per acceptance criterion 1 dependent on additional criteria mentioned above.
3. The software transfers the Pseudo-GOP 88130 or 88131 specified by the user with the ADT billing in field 5001.

### Notes

The software may use the following criteria (per the Guideline of the Federal Joint Committee on the Conduct of Psychotherapy (Psychotherapy Guideline)) to determine whether a relapse prevention can follow:

1. Indication in the patient data whether a relapse prevention is to be carried out after completion of long-term therapy.
2. Treatment duration of the psychotherapy (cf. Psychotherapy Guideline [GBA_RiLi_Psychotherapie], section 14 (3), sentences 1, 2).
3. Age of the insured (cf. Psychotherapy Guideline, section 1 (4), sentence 1).
4. Presence of a mental disability of the insured (cf. Psychotherapy Guideline, section 1 (4), sentence 5).
5. Amount of remaining contingent (cf. Psychotherapy Guideline, section 14 (3), sentences 1, 2).

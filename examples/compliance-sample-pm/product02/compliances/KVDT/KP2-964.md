### Requirement

To support the psychotherapist in recording and transferring a termination notification with Pseudo-GOP 88130 or 88131 via the quarterly billing, the software must be able to calculate the currently existing cross-quarter remaining contingent of an approved psychotherapy.

### Rationale

This requirement results from section 17 para. 7 of the Psychotherapy Agreement (Annex 1 BMV-Ae).

### Acceptance Criteria

1. The software must calculate the currently existing cross-quarter remaining contingent of an approved psychotherapy immediately after service recording and during billing creation.
2. The software must offer the user the ability to mark services already billed in previous quarters that were not accepted by the responsible Kassenaerztliche Vereinigung, so that they are excluded from further calculations of the remaining contingent.

### Notes

The currently existing remaining contingent of an approved psychotherapy is calculated cross-quarter. It is derived from the difference between the approved contingent of psychotherapeutic services as the number of approved therapy units in total and the actually performed therapy units as the number of billed fee schedule positions in total. A fee schedule position corresponds in the normal case to one therapy unit. For group therapy sessions of less than 100 minutes but at least 50 minutes duration (half sessions), which are identified by correspondingly uniformly coded additional identifiers, two identified fee schedule positions correspond to one therapy unit. These half sessions can be recognized by the fact that the point value of the corresponding GOP with letter suffix equals exactly half the point value of the base GOP without letter suffix. The contingent is independent of whether individual therapy, group therapy, or one of the combination treatments is carried out. The consideration of a billed fee schedule position is independent of the chronological order of the service and its approval by the health insurance fund.
